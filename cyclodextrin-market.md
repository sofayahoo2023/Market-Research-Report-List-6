<p><p>The cyclodextrin market is categorized into several types, including alpha-cyclodextrin, beta-cyclodextrin, and gamma-cyclodextrin, each differing in their molecular structure and size. Alpha-cyclodextrin has six glucose units, offering a smaller cavity, ideal for sugars and small molecules. Beta-cyclodextrin, with seven glucose units, accommodates larger guest molecules, while gamma-cyclodextrin, composed of eight units, has the largest cavity for bulkier compounds. Additionally, cyclodextrin derivatives, modified for enhanced solubility and stability, expand their applications across pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics.</p></p>
