<p><p>The Pregnancy-associated Plasma Protein A (PAPP-A) test kit market is segmented into two main types: Time-resolved Immunofluorescence (TRIF) and Chemiluminescence Immunoassay (CLIA). TRIF utilizes fluorescent markers to detect PAPP-A levels with high sensitivity and specificity, making it suitable for early pregnancy screening. In contrast, CLIA employs chemical reactions to produce light, allowing for quantification of PAPP-A in serum. Both methods facilitate prenatal screening for potential complications and are essential tools in obstetric care.</p></p>
