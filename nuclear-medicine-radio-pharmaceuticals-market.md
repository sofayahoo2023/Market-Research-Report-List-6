<p><p>Nuclear medicine utilizes small amounts of radioactive materials for diagnosis and treatment. The market is divided into diagnostic and therapeutic types. Diagnostic medicine includes SPECT radiopharmaceuticals, which create detailed images of organs, and PET radiopharmaceuticals, which identify metabolic processes. Therapeutic medicine involves beta emitters for treating cancer by delivering targeted radiation, alpha emitters that provide localized treatment with higher precision, and brachytherapy isotopes, which involve placing radiation sources close to tumors for effective treatment.</p></p>

<p><p>Nuclear medicine employs radioactive materials, known as radiopharmaceuticals, for diagnostic imaging and therapy. In diagnostic applications, techniques like Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) and Positron Emission Tomography (PET) are widely used. SPECT utilizes gamma rays to create 3D images of physiological processes, while PET measures metabolic activity by detecting positron emissions.
