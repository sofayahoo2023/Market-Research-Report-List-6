<p><p>Urea-Formaldehyde (UF) adhesive resins are commonly categorized into two types in the wood market: Type I and Type II. Type I resins are water-resistant, suitable for interior applications where moisture exposure is minimal. In contrast, Type II resins offer a higher level of water resistance, making them ideal for exterior applications or environments with greater humidity. Both types provide excellent bonding strength and are extensively used in the production of engineered wood products, like plywood and particleboard.</p></p>
