<p><p>The food irradiation market encompasses three main types of radiation: electron beam radiation, gamma radiation, and X-ray radiation. Electron beam radiation uses high-energy electrons to kill bacteria and extend shelf life, offering rapid processing. Gamma radiation, derived from radioactive isotopes, is widely used for its deep penetration and effectiveness in sterilizing food. X-ray radiation also provides deep penetration and is used for pest control and sterilization, appealing to manufacturers seeking efficient preservation methods. Each type serves distinct applications in food safety and quality enhancement.</p></p>
