<p><p>The battery power bank market primarily features four types of batteries. Lithium-Ion (Li-Ion) batteries are popular for their high energy density and longevity. Lithium-Polymer (Li-Polymer) batteries offer flexibility in design and lightweight properties, suited for slim devices. Nickel Metal Hydride (NiMH) batteries provide a balance of capacity and cost, though they have lower energy density compared to lithium options. Lastly, Nickel Cadmium (Ni-Cd) batteries are known for their durability and quick charging but suffer from memory effect and lower energy efficiency.</p></p>
