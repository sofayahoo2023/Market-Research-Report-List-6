<p><p>The Clinical Trials Imaging market encompasses various imaging modalities essential for assessing treatment efficacy and safety during clinical trials. Computed Tomography (CT) offers detailed cross-sectional images, while Ultrasound uses sound waves for real-time imaging. Echocardiography specializes in heart assessments, and Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides high-resolution images of soft tissues. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) detects metabolic processes, and X-Ray offers quick visualization of bones and structures. Other modalities may include specialized techniques like fluoroscopy and nuclear medicine, enhancing diagnostic capabilities in trials.</p></p>
