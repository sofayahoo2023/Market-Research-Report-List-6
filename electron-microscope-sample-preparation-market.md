<p><p>The electron microscope sample preparation market encompasses various techniques to ensure samples are suitable for high-resolution imaging. Ion milling is used to thin samples by removing material ion-atom by ion-atom. Coaters apply conductive layers to prevent charging. Freeze fracture systems prepare samples for observation at cryogenic temperatures, while high-pressure freezers rapidly freeze biological samples. Cryo transfer systems facilitate the handling of frozen samples. Plasma cleaners remove contaminants, and critical point drying systems prepare samples without causing distortion. Other methods include various specialized techniques for specific applications.</p></p>
