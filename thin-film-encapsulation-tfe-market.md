<p><p>Thin-film encapsulation (TFE) is a technology used to protect sensitive electronic components, particularly organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) and organic photovoltaics (OPVs), from environmental degradation. TFE can be classified into two main market types: organic layers and inorganic layers. Organic layers utilize polymers to create barriers against moisture and oxygen, while inorganic layers use materials like metal oxides or ceramics that offer superior protection and durability. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the longevity and efficiency of electronic devices.</p></p>
