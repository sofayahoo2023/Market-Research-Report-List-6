<p><p>The Fitness and Camping Mats market comprises various materials catering to different user needs. XLPE (cross-linked polyethylene) offers lightweight, durable options ideal for insulation. NXLPE enhances performance with better resilience and moisture resistance. EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate) provides cushioning and flexibility, making it popular for fitness applications. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) mats are affordable and versatile but less eco-friendly. Rubbers, including NBR (nitrile butadiene rubber) and natural rubber, offer superior grip and durability. Additionally, mats made from natural materials like fibers and cork are gaining traction for their sustainability.</p></p>
