<p><p>Point-of-Entry Water Treatment Systems encompass various methods for enhancing water quality. Water softening reduces hardness by removing minerals like calcium and magnesium. Filtration eliminates impurities and particles using physical barriers. Reverse Osmosis (RO) employs semipermeable membranes to remove contaminants and dissolved substances. Disinfection uses chemical or physical processes to kill pathogens. Sedimentation allows suspended particles to settle out of water. Distillation involves boiling water and condensing the steam to separate contaminants, ensuring cleaner water at the source.</p></p>
