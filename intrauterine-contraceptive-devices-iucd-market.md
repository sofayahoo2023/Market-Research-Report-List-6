<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCDs) are a form of long-term contraception inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy. The market is divided into three types: non-hormonal IUCDs, which include copper devices that create a hostile environment for sperm; hormonal IUCDs, which release progestin to thicken cervical mucus and inhibit ovulation; and inert IUCDs, which have no active ingredients but are designed to remain in place without affecting hormone levels. Each type offers unique mechanisms for contraception and user preferences.</p></p>
