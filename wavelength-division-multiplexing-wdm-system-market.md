<p><p>Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) systems enhance optical fiber communications by allowing multiple wavelengths (or channels) to be transmitted simultaneously over a single fiber. The market is primarily divided into two types: Coarse Wavelength Division Multiplexing (CWDM) and Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM). CWDM typically uses fewer channels with wider spacing, making it cost-effective for shorter distances. In contrast, DWDM employs closely spaced channels, maximizing capacity for long-distance communication, making it ideal for high-bandwidth applications.</p></p>
